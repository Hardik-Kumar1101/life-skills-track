Sexual harassment is any unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical harassment of a sexual nature in the workplace or learning environment. It can be committed by anyone, regardless of their gender, and can take many forms.

Some common types of behavior that can constitute sexual harassment include:

1 Unwanted touching or physical contact. This can include anything from a pat on the back to groping, and can be done by anyone, regardless of their gender.

2 Unwelcome sexual advances. This can include asking for or demanding sexual favors, or making sexual comments or jokes that are not welcome.

3 Verbal or written harassment of a sexual nature. This can include making sexually suggestive comments, jokes, or gestures; spreading rumors about someone's sexual activity; or displaying sexually explicit images or materials.

4 Creating a hostile work environment. This can include making unwelcome sexual comments or jokes, displaying sexually explicit images or materials, or engaging in other behavior that creates a hostile or offensive work environment.

5 It is important to note that sexual harassment does not have to be explicitly sexual in nature. For example, making derogatory comments about someone's gender or sexual orientation can also be considered sexual harassment.
